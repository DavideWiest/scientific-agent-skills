# Scientific Brainstorming

Adapted from K-Dense's upstream `scientific-brainstorming` skill.

## Workflow

1. Clarify the open problem, desired contribution, constraints, and what is
   already known or tried.
2. Generate varied candidate directions, including conservative baselines,
   alternative mechanisms, failed-path reconsiderations, and cross-domain
   analogies when justified.
3. Mark speculative ideas as speculative. Avoid turning brainstorming into a
   novelty or feasibility claim.
4. Cluster and compare candidates by potential insight, utility, novelty,
   required evidence, cost, risks, and fastest informative test.
5. Move promising directions to `literature-review`,
   `hypothesis-generation`, or `test-hypothesis` before substantial execution.

Capture durable beliefs about promising or dead-end directions only when
supported by meaningful reasoning or evidence.
